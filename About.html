About me

Hi, my name is Leo. Welcome to my website!

I am a first-year MPhil student at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, where I am part of the interstellar medium group under the guidance of Prof. Renbin Yan. Prior to this, I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Hong Kong. I also had the chance to broaden my academic experience through a one-semester exchange program at McGill University.

I am currently exploring PhD opportunities for Fall 2024. While my current research is in a different area, I have a strong passion for working with astrophotonics devices. My interest in this field started during my postgraduate course on photonics integrated circuits, where I learned about the revolutionary impact of these devices. Later, while reading the book "Principle of astrophotonics," I discovered their importance in future astronomical instruments. I am fascinated by this field and hope to be a part of it in the future. Through my MPhil and BSc studies, I have gained skills in optical testing, results analysis, and astronomical instrumentation that will be valuable for PhD research in astrophotonics.

You can find out more about my current research in the Research or Instrumentation page.
